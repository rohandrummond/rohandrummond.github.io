Understanding my identity, values, strengths and limitations

DEALING WITH ETHICAL DECISIONS

My partner and I recently spent a month in Istanbul, Turkey. As we were walking back to our apartment one evening we noticed a couple arguing. We soon realised that the woman was trying to leave and the man was refusing. He wasn't being violent, but kept blocking her path and at one point even lifted her back to where she had been standing.

Our instinct was to check whether she needed help but we found ourselves in a dilemma - intervene and cause a misunderstanding, or leave and continue with our day. We couldn't speak Turkish, were worried about overstepping cultural boundaries/social norms, and weren't sure if the man would get violent if we stepped in.

We decided to approach the woman. Our thought process was that it was better for us to check and her say it was fine, than for us to walk away if it wasn't. She nervously laughed it off and said she was fine, so we left feeling that there wasn't much more we could do.

Although at the time I was unsure, reflecting on the decision now I think we made the right choice. We all assume we won't fall victim to the bystander effect, but when you're in the moment it's not so simple.

MY CULTURE AND THE PEOPLE AROUND ME

Born in Aotearoa, I'm half Fijian-Indian and half New Zealand European. My family and cultures have played a huge part in shaping the person that I am today.

My mum immigrated from Fiji when she was 16, with no family or friends in the country. She worked part-time at the supermarket to to pay board with a family in Wellington while she went to high school. My dad was born and raised in Auckland. With no formal tertiary education he eventually found himself as a cable runner at TVNZ, by the end of his career he was retiring as the Director of Broadcasting for SKY TV. My parents and grandparents passed down knowledge and values that are at the core of who I am today.

I took some time to take a step back and reflect on what my values are, including tests on personalvalu.es and azimuthpsych.com. Deep down I think I already had a pretty clear view on what is important to me, but it was a great reminder of what motivates me in life.

Work hard, take care of your family, apply yourself, be kind and compassionate, strive for excellence, look after your health, live life to the fullest, and enjoy it (all cheesy but true). It's only during writing this that it really sunk in just how much I am a product of my environment, and I'm grateful that this was the environment I was raised in.

MY STRENGTHS AND HOW THEY WILL SUPPORT ME

Being mindful of my strengths (and limitations) will be a big part of ensuring that my learning journey is efficient, enjoyable and meaningful.

Again I think that these were things that I inherently knew, but it was helpful to refresh myself on what comes naturally to me and where I can improve. Above all I think my biggest strength is my passion for software and desire to pursue it as a career. I took the DOPE Personality test on richardstep.com, the VIA Strength Test and The Big Five test. My DOPE result was the Dove, who is considered "people-orientated, loyal, friendly, hard-working and a great team player". This and the other tests helped me pin down 5 key traits that I consider strengths.

Humility keeps me open to feedback and stops my ego from getting in the way of learning. Integrity ensures I am honest with myself about how I am progressing and whether I am giving 100% effort. Perseverance will help me push through challenges and difficult times. Diligence means showing up everyday and applying myself to the best of my ability. Communication will make group learning more productive and enjoyable.

MY LIMITATIONS AND HOW THESE MIGHT AFFECT ME

I'm a big believer that identifying our weaknesses and areas for improvement is as important (if not more important) than our strengths. My aim is to be honest and mindful about these areas, so that I can stay present, focused and productive during the course.

Coming back to my DOPE Personality result (the Dove) - while they have some great traits they also tend to avoid change, confrontation, risk-taking and assertiveness. In addition to this I've identified some key areas where I can improve.

I am working on getting used to the “just in time” learning model, and often get tempted to deep-dive into topics and get sidetracked. Being a perfectionist is something that I struggle with, and can result in me sinking a lot of time into tasks that aren't high value. I'm naturally introverted, so I'd like to manage this and prevent it from getting in the way of applying myself. Lastly I'd like to try working on approaching life in general with more appreciation, excitement and energy.

WORKING PRODUCTIVELY WITH OTHERS

In a previous job I was responsible for managing a monthly billing process, capturing all of the costs that needed to be invoiced across our clients. The challenge was that some team members repeatedly missed deadlines, which put pressure on the whole team.

I raised this with them several times but had no luck. My first approach was to send out a group reminder every month, a week ahead of our due date. Unfortunately after several months with little improvement I started to take ownership of the additional billing work.

At a surface level it felt like it was working, we were meeting our deadlines and I developed a lot of experience in this space. However I soon realised that it was more like a plaster that masked the symptoms of a bigger underlying issue. Workload and technical knowledge was not being spread across everyone, which left the team vulnerable if I wasn't there (and put more pressure on me).

In hindsight, I think it would have been better to escalate the challenge to management earlier so we could work on a longer-term, more sustainable solution.
