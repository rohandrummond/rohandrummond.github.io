The house with 4 walls

Te Whare Tapa Whā

In my experience, many modern approaches to health feel rigid and narrow - count your calories, go to the gym, get your daily Headspace meditation in etc. After learning about Te Whare Tapa Whā it feels like we’ve gone backwards, and we only need to look not so far back in Aotearoa’s history for a more meaningful perspective on how to approach our well-being.

WHAT IS TE WHARE TAPA WHĀ?

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori perspective of a person's wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984. It includes 4 areas of health, represented as the four walls of wharenui (meeting house), with whenua (the natural world) as it's foundation.

Engaging with the concept of neuroplasticity looks different for everyone, but some of the overarching principles that are important to keep top of mind are:

  • Taha tinana: Physical wellbeing
  • Taha hinengaro: Mental & Emotional wellbeing
  • Taha wairua: Spiritual wellbeing
  • Taha whānau: Family & Social wellbeing

TAHA TINANA

How my body moves, feels and changes

  • Eat healthy foods throughout the week that give me energy and make me feel good
  • Stay consistent with my morning workout routine throughout the week

TAHA HINENGARO

My thoughts, feelings and how I communicate

  • Set aside 5 minutes every morning for journalling
  • Complete 5 minute meditations, 3 times a week

TAHA WAIRUA

My values and the way that I find meaning

  • Spend time exploring and learning about different philosophies
  • Once a week take 5 minutes to reflect on my values, purpose and goals

TAHA WHĀNAU

The connection and sense of belonging I get from my community

  • Dedicated quality time with my partner each night
  • Cook for the family once a week

WHENUA

The connection to our land which supports everything in life

  • Continue volunteering for Manta Watch NZ
  • Get out in nature once a week e.g. beach, bush walk

I'm still learning to look after my wellbeing in a holistic way and take care of all 4 walls of Te Whare Tapa Whā. I found this exercise really interesting and enlightening, and I look forward to incorporating more Te Ao Māori principles into my life moving forward.
