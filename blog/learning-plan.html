My learning plan

Setting expectations and intentions for Dev Academy

MY LONG TERM CAREER GOAL

The goal is to pursue a career in Engineering, working with technologies and products that I feel passionately about. At this stage I would prefer to take a technical route as opposed to a managerial path.

MY STRENGTHS AND LIMITATIONS

My biggest strength is my passion for software and drive to pursue it as a career. Other traits that I think will serve me well during my learning journey are humility, integrity, perseverance, diligence, and communication.

At the same time I need to be mindful of my limitations. I lean towards being a perfectionist, tend to avoid risk and confrontation, get tempted to deep-dive into topics that aren’t necessarily productive, and would consider myself naturally introverted. I’m hoping that by identifying these traits early I can proactively work on them and ensure I’m applying myself and getting the most out of this experience.

SKILLS I‘D LIKE TO DEVELOP

While there are always new technical skills to learn and that is something that excites me, I think the main gap for me is practical experience coding in a team environment. I’m looking forward to honing this area of my skillset so I can efficiently work with other developers on projects.

WORKLOAD AND COLLABORATION

I’m currently managing my workload by setting clear boundaries in my daily routine around what time will be dedicated to Dev Academy. I'm also setting clear goals each day, making sure to not over-commit so I can focus on quality rather than fast learning.

I plan on collaborating with other students, helping each other where we can and facilitating the teina/teina learning approach mentioned in the prep curriculum. We all come from different backgrounds and are at different stages of the learning journey, and I’m committed to respecting everyone whether they are learner, facilitator or industry representative.

If I feel that things are starting to get too much, I will take a step back to prioritise and ask for guidance where needed. I plan on taking regular breaks to prevent burnout, and will be optimising things like sleep and diet to ensure I am operating at my best.

SEEKING HELP

If I find myself struggling I will find a balance between trying to figure something out myself (since this is an essential part of being a programmer), and being honest with myself about when I need help. When I do need to reach out to a facilitator, I will post my question in the #foundations-helpdesk Discord channel using the template provided in the prep curriculum, or if appropriate directly message my facilitator.

FACILITATOR EXPECTATIONS

I expect the facilitation team to guide and challenge me throughout this journey, to make sure that I am getting the most out of the experience and leaving as prepared as I can be to enter the industry. At the same time I acknowledge they have their own workload and lives going on, so I will be respectful and understanding of their other commitments.

OTHER COMMITMENTS

I’m extremely fortunate that I have no other strict time commitments at this time. While I plan to remain focused on taking care of myself mentally and physically, Dev Academy is my sole focus and my aim is to make the most of this experience.
