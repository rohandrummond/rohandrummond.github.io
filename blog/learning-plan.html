My learning plan

Setting expectations and intentions for Dev Academy

MY LONG TERM CAREER GOAL

I would like to pursue a career in engineering, working with technologies and products that I feel passionately about. At this stage I would prefer to take a technical route to Senior Engineer and above, rather than a managerial route into Engineering management.

MY STRENGHTS AND LIMITATIONS

My biggest strength is my passion for software and drive to pursue it as a career. Other traits that I think will serve me well during my learning journey are humility, integrity, perseverance, diligence, and communication.

At the same time I need to be mindful of my limitations. I lean towards being a perfectionist, tend to avoid risk and confrontation, get tempted to deep-dive into topics that aren’t necessarily productive, and would consider myself naturally introverted. I’m hoping that by identifying these traits early I can proactively working on them I can make sure that I’m applying myself and getting the most out of this experience.

SKILLS I‘D LIKE TO DEVELOP

hile there are always new technical skills to learn and that is something that excites me, I think the main gap in my current experience is being able to code in a team environment that resembles what is required in real industry roles. I’m looking forward to honing this area of my skillset so I can efficiently work with other developers on projects.

MANAGING MY WORKLOAD

I’m currently managing my workload by setting clear boundaries in my daily routine around what time will be dedicated to Dev Academy. I am setting clear goals each day, making sure to not over-commit so I can focus on quality rather than fast learning.

I plan on collaborating with other students, helping each other where we can and facilitating the teina/teina learning approach mentioned in the prep curriculum. We all come from different backgrounds and stages in life, and I’m committed to respecting everyone whether they are learner, facilitator or industry representative.

If I feel that things are starting to get too much, I will take a step back to prioritise and ask for guidance where needed. I plan on taking regularl breaks to prevent burnout, and will be optimising things like sleep and diet to ensure I am operating at my best.

SEEKING HELP

If I find myself struggling I will find a balance between trying to figure something out myself (since this is an essential part of being a programmer), and being honest with myself about when I need help. When I do need to reach out to a facilitator, I will post my question in the #foundations-helpdesk Discord channel using the template provided in the prep curriculum, or if appropriate directly message my facilitator.

FACILITATOR EXPECTATIONS

I expect the facilitation team to guide and challenge me throughout the learning process, ensuring that I am getting the most out of the programme and leaving as prepared as I can be to enter the industry. At the same time I acknowledge they have their own workload and lives going on, so I will be respectful and understanding of their other commitments.

OTHER COMMITMENTS

I’m fortunate enough to have no other commitments at this point in my life. While I plan to remain focused on taking care of myself mentally and physically, Dev Academy is my sole focus and I plan to make the most of this experience.
